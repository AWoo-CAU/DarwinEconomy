Northern Territory Economic Outlook

The Territory has consistently outperformed recent fiscal forecasts, maintaining steady growth in a high-inflation environment and preparing for a high-growth phase driven by major energy and infrastructure pipelines.

Housing

Darwin’s Tight Rental Market

Greater Darwin has faced a persistently tight rental market since 2020, with vacancy rates worsening toward the end of 2025 to levels well below 1%. This is in comparison to 2019 when units were almost 9% vacancy rates.

Defence

The Top End is now recognized as a critical region to Australia's national defence strategy, serving as the nation's primary forward operating hub.

ADF & US Military Alliance

Host to permanent Army, Navy, and Airforce assets, and the central pillar of the US strategic alliance.

April 2026 Spending Announcement

Commonwealth Government announced an additional $53Bn in defence spending for Northern Bases.

This historic investment cements the NT's role in the 'Strategy of Denial'.

Energy and Gas: Key Milestones

The energy sector remains the primary driver of the NT’s export-led growth model.

Barossa Offshore Project

The $6 billion project is winding down construction; production expected to commence January 2026.

Beetaloo Sub-Basin

Onshore gas is progressing rapidly, with Tamboran Resources expecting "first gas" in early 2026. The Shenandoah South Pilot and Carpentaria Gas Project reach key milestones.
